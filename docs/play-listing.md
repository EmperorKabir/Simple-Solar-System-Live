## Full description
**Field cap: 4000 characters. Below: ~1100 chars including formatting.**

```
A simple, clean 3D solar-system orrery that shows where every major planet and its biggest moons are RIGHT NOW.

KEY FEATURES
• Live positions for all 8 planets, the Sun, the Moon, and the major moons of Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto.
• Time travel — jump backward or forward by hour, day, week, month, year, or decade with simple +/- buttons. Hold any button for 4-jumps-per-second auto-repeat to watch orbits unfold.
• Tap a planet, moon, or the Sun to fly the camera to it.
• Pinch and drag to explore the system in 3D.
• Live time-zone display in your local zone (with a 30+ time-zone picker if needed).
• Built-in user guide that explains everything in plain language.

ACCURACY
Positions are computed from real published ephemerides (NASA JPL Horizons + Meeus astronomical algorithms) and match real-world planetary positions to within about a degree. Moon distances inside each system are kept proportional to reality where possible. Planet-to-Sun distances are visually compressed so the whole system fits on one screen.

OFFLINE & PRIVATE
The app makes ZERO network requests. It collects no data, requests no permissions, has no ads, no tracking, no analytics. Open source — see the linked privacy policy / GitHub repo.

BEST FOR
Casual stargazers, students learning the layout of the solar system, telescope users wanting a quick "where is Jupiter right now" reference, and anyone who likes watching the planets move.

This app is an indicative guide, not a precision astronomical instrument. For sub-arcsecond positions, use a professional ephemeris tool.
```
